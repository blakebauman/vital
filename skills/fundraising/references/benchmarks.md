# Early-stage fundraising benchmarks

**Last verified: 2025-Q4. Treat every number here as stale.**

These are starting anchors, not answers. Round sizes, valuations, and dilution
move with the market, and they move fastest exactly when a founder is raising.
**Re-research any figure before you quote it to a founder if the "last verified"
date above is more than ~6 months old**, and cite the source you found rather
than this file. If you cannot verify a number, give the founder the shape of the
answer (what drives it) instead of a stale figure presented as current.

They also describe the US venture market. Other geographies differ materially on
round size, instrument, and dilution norms — say so rather than transplanting
these.

## Pre-seed

- **Amount** — ~$250K–$1.5M; up to ~$2M for strong or AI-native teams.
- **Instrument** — almost always a post-money SAFE, cap ~$5M–$15M.
- **Dilution** — ~10–18% combined.
- **What investors expect** — working prototype, first 10–50 users, a validated
  problem (on the order of 50+ discovery interviews), founding team assembled.

## Seed

- **Amount** — ~$2.5M–$5M; ~$6M–$10M for AI and deep-tech.
- **Valuation** — post-money median around ~$24M, commonly ~$20M–$30M.
- **Dilution** — ~18–25%, more with stacked SAFEs; often includes a 10–15%
  option pool created at the round.
- **Instrument** — roughly half SAFEs, half priced; priced rounds dominate above
  ~$3M.
- **What investors expect** — a working product with real users and early
  metrics (e.g. $5K–$50K MRR for SaaS) plus retention signals.

## How to use these

1. Look up the current figure and cite it.
2. Fall back to the range above only as a sanity check on what you found.
3. Anchor the recommendation to the **milestone the raise buys**, not to the
   benchmark. The benchmark tells you whether the ask is unusual; the milestone
   tells you whether it is right.
